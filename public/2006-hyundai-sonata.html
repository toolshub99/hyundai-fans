2006 Hyundai Sonata: Complete Review, Fuel Economy, and Value Analysis

Discover the redesigned 2006 Hyundai Sonata with its refined styling, improved performance, and exceptional value that competes strongly in the midsize sedan segment.

2006 Hyundai Sonata: Complete Redesign

The 2006 Hyundai Sonata represents a significant evolution in Hyundai's midsize sedan lineup with an all-new platform, refined styling, and improved performance. This complete redesign introduces Hyundai's commitment to quality and value, creating a competitive offering in the midsize sedan market.

Built on Hyundai's new NF platform, the 2006 Sonata offers improved driving dynamics, enhanced safety, and increased interior space. The cabin features quality materials and thoughtful technology that delivers impressive value for the price.

Key Features of the 2006 Redesign

  • Completely new platform with increased dimensions
  • Refined and sophisticated exterior styling
  • Available V6 engine with 235 horsepower
  • Standard electronic stability control
  • Six standard airbags
  • Spacious and well-appointed interior

Hyundai Sonata LX: Premium Features at a Great Value

The Hyundai Sonata LX trim offers an exceptional balance of premium features and value. Positioned between the base GL and premium GLS trims, the LX adds desirable comfort and convenience features without a significant price increase.

LX Exclusive Features:

  • 16-inch alloy wheels
  • Power driver seat with lumbar support
  • Leather-wrapped steering wheel and shift knob
  • Automatic climate control
  • Fog lights
  • Steering wheel audio controls

Respectable Fuel Economy: Hyundai Sonata MPG

The 2006 Sonata delivers competitive fuel efficiency for a midsize sedan, with the base four-cylinder engine providing a great balance of power and economy. The available V6 model offers strong performance while maintaining reasonable fuel consumption.

Engine/Trim City MPG Highway MPG Combined MPG
2.4L 4-cylinder (GL, LX) 24 34 28
3.3L V6 (GLS) 20 30 24

The Sonata's fuel economy numbers were competitive for its class in 2006, with the four-cylinder model offering particularly good efficiency for daily commuting and highway driving.

2006 Hyundai Sonata Pricing & Trim Levels

The 2006 Hyundai Sonata offers exceptional value in the competitive midsize sedan segment. With a starting price significantly below many competitors, the Sonata delivers premium features, solid performance, and refined styling at an accessible price point.

Advanced Technology & Safety Features

The 2006 Sonata came well-equipped with standard safety features that were often optional on competitors. Electronic Stability Control and six airbags were standard across all trims - a significant advantage in its class.

Standard Safety Features:

  • Electronic Stability Control (ESC)
  • Traction Control System (TCS)
  • Anti-lock Braking System (ABS)
  • Six airbags (front, side, curtain)
  • Active front head restraints

Available premium features included leather upholstery, heated seats, a sunroof, and an upgraded audio system with CD changer.

Why Choose the 2006 Hyundai Sonata?

The 2006 Hyundai Sonata stands out in the midsize sedan segment with its refined styling, comprehensive standard safety features, and exceptional value. Its combination of attractive design, comfortable interior, and competitive pricing made it a compelling choice for sedan shoppers in 2006.

Whether you're considering the well-equipped base GL model, the feature-rich LX, or the premium GLS with V6 power, the Sonata delivers a quality experience that's difficult to match in its price range.
